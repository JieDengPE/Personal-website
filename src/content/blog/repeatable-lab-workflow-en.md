When projects become hardware-heavy, documentation stops being optional overhead and becomes part of the engineering system itself.

I like separating three kinds of writing:

1. Reflective notes about why a direction matters.
2. Project notes about what changed and what was observed.
3. Manuals that tell someone exactly how to reproduce a setup.

That separation makes it easier to think clearly and to find information later. It is also the reason this website keeps blog posts and manuals as two different sections.
